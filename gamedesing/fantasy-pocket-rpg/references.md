# Reference Pack

## Synthesis

Borrow the feeling of a large fantasy adventure, but implement it as a compact mobile/PC RPG built from map nodes, events, first-person/illustrated encounters, loot, and a safe camp loop.

### Adopt

- Province map with clear points of interest and danger tiers.
- First-person/illustrated adventure screens for ruins, roads, caves, shrines, and towns.
- Camp as rest/craft/companion/perk hub between expeditions.
- Simple but visible stats, resources, loot rarity, and reputation.
- Dragon thread as long-term progression and world mystery.

### Avoid

- Full Skyrim open world scope.
- Settlement/base management as the main loop.
- Heavy survival micromanagement that buries the fantasy.
- Live-service dependency or opaque monetization.
- Copying exact Elder Scrolls races, names, UI, icons, guilds, or lore.

## Fake Shot Requirement From Refs

The first fake shot should show: fantasy map or first-person ruins, hero stats, inventory/loot reward, active quest, camp button locked/unlocked by safe state, companion/draconic mystery hint, and one clear action path.

## First-Slice Mechanic From Refs

The player enters old forest ruins, resolves one encounter, finds a dragon-marked relic, returns to a safe campfire, crafts/rests/levels one perk, talks to a companion, then unlocks the next map node.
