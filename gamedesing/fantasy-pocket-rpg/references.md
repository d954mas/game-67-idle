# Reference Pack

## Synthesis

Borrow the feeling of a large fantasy adventure, but implement it as a compact mobile/PC RPG built from map nodes, first-person illustrated encounters, short fights, loot, and a safe camp loop.

## Adopt

- Province map with visible routes, danger tiers, and locked next nodes.
- First-person/illustrated screens for ruins, roads, caves, shrines, towns.
- Turn-based or choice-based combat that can be completed quickly.
- Camp as rest/craft/companion/perk hub between expeditions.
- Simple visible resources: Health, Resolve, Supplies, Gold, Herbs.
- Dragon thread as long-term mystery and hero identity.

## Avoid

- Full open-world scope.
- Settlement/base management as the main loop.
- Heavy survival micromanagement.
- MMO/gacha clutter.
- Direct copying of Elder Scrolls, Fallout, D&D, or other protected names, races, symbols, UI layouts, factions, or lore.

## First-Slice Translation From Refs

The player enters old forest ruins, resolves one encounter, finds a dragon-marked relic, returns to a safe campfire, crafts/rests/levels one perk, talks to a companion, then unlocks the next map node.

## Fake Shot Requirement From Refs

The first visual proof must show: fantasy map or first-person ruins, hero stats, active quest, action choices, loot/reward feedback, camp locked/unlocked by safe state, companion/draconic mystery hint, and a clear next action.
