# Progression Plan

## Goal

Define how the game grows from a tiny prototype into a larger RPG while keeping the same core loop.

## Progression Layers

### 1. Player Power

Player power answers: "Can I handle stronger routes?"

Systems:

- Health max.
- Resolve max.
- weapon damage;
- armor mitigation;
- consumable strength;
- perks;
- special dragon-linked abilities later.

First slice:

- `Old Knife`: 5 damage.
- `Trail Herbalist I`: potion heal 10 -> 15.

Future expansion:

- weapon traits, not many raw numbers;
- armor with simple resistance tags;
- perks that change action choices;
- dragon powers as rare milestone unlocks.

### 2. World Access

World access answers: "Where can I go next?"

Unlock types:

- route unlocked;
- safe camp discovered;
- hazard cleared;
- faction permit gained;
- companion route revealed;
- dragon seal opened.

First slice:

- `Hunter's Ford` unlocks after the camp beat.

Future expansion:

- region maps with 8-12 nodes;
- danger tiers;
- optional side nodes;
- region climax route;
- hidden route from companion or relic.

### 3. Camp Capability

Camp capability answers: "How do I prepare better?"

Camp upgrades must stay light:

- better rest;
- more crafting recipes;
- gear repair;
- companion talk;
- perk selection;
- relic inspection;
- route planning.

Forbidden:

- rooms;
- workers;
- construction timers;
- settlement economy.

### 4. Companion Trust

Companion trust answers: "Who is with me and why do they matter?"

Progression:

- first practical advice;
- route hint;
- personal quest;
- combat or camp utility;
- conflict/loyalty choice;
- late-game bond outcome.

Companion utility should be simple:

- reveals route;
- improves check chance;
- reduces camp cost;
- adds unique dialogue;
- opens quest resolution option.

### 5. Dragon Mystery

Dragon mystery answers: "What is happening to the hero?"

Milestones:

1. Shard/mark.
2. Omen dreams.
3. Relic resonance.
4. Spirit/egg trace.
5. First controlled power.
6. Region-level dragon reveal.

Rules:

- no dragon pet at start;
- no dragon collection;
- each milestone must unlock a new decision, route, or combat/camp option;
- dragon progress should feel rare and meaningful.

### 6. Faction Reputation

Faction reputation answers: "How does the province react?"

Use lightly:

- local settlement;
- hunters/rangers;
- ruin scholars;
- cult/drake oath faction;
- merchants.

First use:

- faction hints in region 1, not full system in P0.

Future use:

- route access;
- shop service;
- companion conflict;
- alternate quest endings.

## Progression Cadence

### First 5 Minutes

- route unlock;
- first loot;
- first camp use;
- first perk/craft improvement.

### First 20 Minutes

- 3-4 nodes;
- 2 enemy types;
- first companion route hint;
- first visible dragon mystery step.

### First Region

- 8-12 nodes;
- 2 camps/safe points;
- 1 mini-boss;
- 1 faction choice;
- 2 companion beats;
- first dragon milestone.

## Design Risks

- Too many progression layers too early.
- Camp growth becoming base-building.
- Dragon progression becoming a collectible system.
- Gear becoming stat clutter.

## Validation

- Player can name the next goal after each session.
- Every progression layer has a visible UI signal.
- First 20 minutes unlock at least one new route, one new ability/perk, and one story clue.
