# Content Planning

Reusable rules for planning game content without losing the core loop.

## Goal

Content should create new player decisions, visible progression, fresh rewards,
or stronger fantasy. More items, levels, upgrades, or screens do not help if
they do not change what the player understands, wants, or can do.

## Content Roles

- Teach: introduces a mechanic or rule.
- Reward: makes progress visible or desirable.
- Pace: controls session rhythm and anticipation.
- Variety: changes context, input, risk, reward, or fantasy.
- Goal: gives the player a reason to continue.
- Expression: lets the player personalize or identify with progress.
- Proof: shows that the game world has changed.

## Core Principles

- Add content only after naming its job in the loop or progression.
- Early content should be small, clear, and high-signal.
- Reuse structures; vary meaning, reward, pacing, or visuals.
- Do not introduce a new content type before the player understands the old one.
- Locked content should motivate without cluttering the current screen.
- Each content batch needs at least one screenshot-worthy moment.
- Plan content around player-visible milestones, not file counts.
- Cut or hide content that weakens the first-session path.

## GDD Checklist

- What player behavior does this content create?
- Which loop or progression layer does it support?
- What is the first example the player sees?
- What makes the second example different?
- What is the reward for completing, buying, collecting, or discovering it?
- What visual change proves completion?
- What is locked, and what unlocks it?
- What text, icon, art, animation, and audio are required?
- What is the minimum content set for a playable test?
- What content can be safely cut without breaking the loop?

## Content Matrix Columns

- ID.
- Player-facing name.
- Type.
- Unlock condition.
- Cost or requirement.
- Reward.
- Visible result.
- Feedback.
- Reuse group or template.
- Validation note.
- Cut priority.

## Anti-Patterns

- Content exists because the screen looked empty.
- Ten items differ only by price and number.
- Content is visible before it has meaning.
- New content adds copy but no new decision or reward.
- The first session shows the full content library instead of the next useful thing.
- Art production starts before content roles and states are defined.
- Content count is treated as readiness even when the first minutes are weak.

## Validation

- Each early content item has a player-visible job.
- At least one content item changes the world, character, board, or UI.
- The first content batch can be tested with a short scripted playthrough.
- A tester can tell why two adjacent items are different.
- Removing low-priority content does not break the first loop.
- Content unlock order supports FTUE, core loop, and meta pacing.
