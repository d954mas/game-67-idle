# Story World Lore Digest

Source packet:
[sources/story_world_reference_packet_2026-07-04.md](sources/story_world_reference_packet_2026-07-04.md)

Status: draft narrative/world direction. This is strong enough to align GDD
docs and discuss content, but not enough to unlock reference-driven gameplay or
final art implementation without later screenshot/video proof.

## Current Canon

- Player-facing title: `Дракон не вернулся`.
- Subtitle / theme statement: `Мир без Дракона`.
- Jam act: `Акт I: Лишний свидетель`.
- Format: 2D illustrated browser RPG with screen flow:
  hub -> map -> location -> autobattle -> reward -> clue/journal -> hub.
- Player role: ordinary seeker/mercenary, not chosen one and not dragon heir.
- Core mystery: the Dragon did not simply vanish; someone prepared for a world
  without him.

## Narrative Thesis

The Great Dragon used to be the world's protective infrastructure. Without him,
cosmic danger appears first as ordinary civic failure: closed roads, empty grain
stores, nervous officials, permits, guards, contract boards, and people who
cannot safely leave town.

The player starts by solving a practical job for money. The story becomes larger
only because the job exposes a clue that should have stayed buried.

## Pillars For Story And World

1. Absence has logistics: every major scene should show what fails when the
   Dragon is gone.
2. Heroism starts as paid survival: the player helps because the job pays, then
   stays because they know too much.
3. Every location carries three layers: local need, visible danger, hidden clue.
4. The Dragon is absent but active in meaning: protector, border seal, public
   myth, political secret.
5. Dark fantasy stays readable: stylized dread, clear silhouettes, short text,
   no realistic misery spiral.

## Act I Shape

| Beat | Name | Function | Story Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `Допуск за ворота` | Teach equipment and first autobattle through an in-world permit. | The seeker earns legal access beyond Last Post. |
| 2 | `Хлеб для Поста` | First real contract: clear the old mill and recover grain. | The player expects food/safety, not conspiracy. |
| 3 | Mill basement clue | Reveal Black Sun signs, cut grain sacks, dead cultist, burned chain bracket, order scrap. | The mill was staged by people, not only monsters. |
| 4 | `Не показывай это никому` | Return to Last Post; officials react with fear. | The clue becomes dangerous knowledge. |
| 5 | `Те, кто пришёл ночью` | Assassination or theft attempt in/near the hub. | The player becomes the extra witness. |
| 6 | `Лесная дорога` | Follow movement of the hidden cargo. | The conspiracy has routes and logistics. |
| 7 | `Тайник Чёрного Солнца` | Act I final location. | The cult prepared for the Dragon's disappearance. |

Act I should end with a clean hook, not a full answer: `Дракон не просто исчез.
Кто-то заранее готовился к миру без него.`

## Factions

- `Совет Маяков`: public order, permits, beacon authority, reward for Dragon
  information. They may hide truths to prevent panic.
- `Гильдия Искателей`: practical contract labor. They give tokens, jobs, rumors,
  and dead-seeker evidence.
- `Культ Чёрного Солнца`: believes the Dragon imprisoned the world or blocked
  a desired power. In Act I they should appear through signs, orders, and
  logistics before speeches.
- `Обычные жители Поста`: food, safety, repairs, grief. They make the cosmic
  problem human-scale.
- Future factions like vampire houses stay visible only as locked-map promise,
  not first-act burden.

## Tone And Language

Use concrete, civic words before myth words:

- contract, token, permit, gate, grain, debt, road, witness, seal, beacon;
- black candles, burned bracket, torn order, ash mark, white-gold light;
- short NPC lines that imply fear through practical concern.

Avoid starting with:

- prophecy, chosen bloodline, cosmic exposition, ancient genealogies, many
  named gods, faction encyclopedias.

## Reference Lessons

- `user-provided` V0 is the main canon source. External refs only sharpen how to
  package it.
- `observed` from browser RPG refs: the first act should be screen-led and
  quest-led, with journal/map/hub doing narrative work.
- `observed` from professional/mercenary refs: ordinary work plus risk/reward is
  stronger for this player role than destiny.
- `observed` from dark narrative refs: secrets should live in institutions,
  places, and items, not only monologues.
- `inferred` design rule: every first-act scene should answer one practical
  question and raise one Dragon mystery question.
