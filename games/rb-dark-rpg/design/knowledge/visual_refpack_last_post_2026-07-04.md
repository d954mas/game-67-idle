# Last Post Visual Refpack First Pass

## Scope

Mode: quick visual reference pass.

This packet is enough to generate exploratory background candidates for the
first hub screen, but it is not a final implementation-ready art bible.

Target screen:

- master/background guide: `1280x700`;
- central game screen: `960x540`, origin `(160,80)`;
- important objects stay inside the central screen;
- top-left `112x72` inside the screen is reserved for Poki;
- first action is `talk_to_guard`, so the gate/guard area must be visible
  without portrait pan;
- background only: no baked NPC, no UI, no text, no logo.

Source-first result: local asset search for `dark fantasy Roblox-like fortified
village gate background hub` returned 0 matches. No sourced image is accepted as
an asset. References are used only for form, lighting, UI density, and
readability.

Accepted art-lead correction: the camera is inside `Last Post`, on the city
side of the gate/checkpoint. `Last Post` is a living city on the border, not
ruins and not a collapsed settlement. It should feel harsh, guarded, and
frontier-worn, but inhabited: repaired walls, lit windows, working gates,
watch posts, market/storage props, patrol routes, and signs of daily life.
The outside danger may be visible only as a narrow view, glow, fog, or horizon
through/above the gate; the primary scene is the inner city space.

Art-lead acceptance: the style direction is approved as a hit. Candidate 05 is
the kept background direction because it fixes the gate exit: the gate opens
toward uncertain wilderness/frontier danger, not toward a castle, second city
district, or heroic landmark. Production should keep Candidate 05's outside
read while recovering more of Candidate 04's inhabited inner-city props and
warm working-border-city feeling.

## Recommended Direction

Use a hybrid direction:

> Blocky dark-fantasy browser-RPG hub: Roblox-like chunky forms and simple
> silhouettes, rendered as a 2D illustrated living frontier city hub with old
> browser RPG readability.

Practical blend for the first candidate:

- Deepwoken: fog and danger beyond the walls, visible only through the gate or
  skyline, dark Roblox-fantasy gate mood.
- Shadovia: simple large gate target and paired warm lights.
- Vesteria/World Zero: chunky readable Roblox RPG forms and functional town
  staging.
- AdventureQuest Worlds: 2D browser RPG layering and clickable hub grammar.
- Darkest Dungeon/Drakensang: ash, ember, grim frontier mood, but not their
  exact rendering style or ruin-first tone.

Composition:

- Center play area: the inner side of a city gate/checkpoint with one obvious
  guard position inside the city, near the passage.
- Eye path: foreground dirt/cobblestone path leads from lower center toward the
  guard/checkpoint and branches into inhabited streets, stalls, storage, or
  patrol routes.
- Left/mid-left: keep top-left Poki reserve visually calm; no key object there.
- Mid-right or lower center-right: guard hotspot area, lit by a lantern or
  brazier, with enough empty readable shape behind it for an overlay sprite.
- Background: repaired inner walls, watchtower backs, dim houses, working
  storage sheds, shuttered stalls, alleys, rooflines, dormant dragon beacon
  silhouette if needed.
- Bleed: trees, wall continuation, mist, rooftops, weathered banners, cliffs;
  decorative only.

Palette:

- main darks: charcoal blue, smoky violet, muted black-green;
- settlement warmth: amber torchlight and window light;
- dragon memory: small white-gold accents, not dominant;
- cult/omen: sparse violet-black marks or distant glow;
- corruption: restrained green-black fog near edges or beyond the gate.

Shape language:

- blocky/tapered roofs, chunky stone blocks, broad wooden beams;
- oversized readable props: gate beams, lanterns, crates, barrels, hoist,
  awnings, contract board silhouette;
- no thin realistic detail in the important guard/gate area;
- background detail drops quickly with depth.

Acceptance criteria for first background candidate:

- no UI, text, logos, watermark, baked NPC, or baked tutorial finger;
- central `960x540` reads without the bleed;
- `112x72` Poki area stays nonessential;
- guard hotspot area is obvious even before the guard sprite is added;
- first tap target can be highlighted without fighting the background;
- portrait pan can crop side bleed without hiding the gate/guard hotspot.

Border-post readability rule:

- The gate/checkpoint must read as the inner side of a border gate, not as an
  arch between two districts of the same city.
- Beyond/through the gate there should be a clear change of world state: outer
  fog, road to the frontier, wall exterior, distant hostile land, watch fires,
  empty patrol road, or dark skyline.
- Do not show a dense continuation of cozy city streets directly past the gate;
  that turns the scene into an internal city passage and weakens `Last Post`.
- Do not show a clear destination castle, keep, or heroic city on the horizon.
  The gate should open toward an uncertain dangerous world, not toward the next
  attractive landmark.

Game-background readability rule:

- This is a game background, not a standalone painting. Prefer large readable
  shape groups over scenic detail.
- Use broad wall planes, big roof masses, chunky crates/barrels, simple lantern
  pools, and clear empty interaction zones.
- Reduce micro-detail: fewer individual stones, beams, windows, roof cuts,
  market props, puddle highlights, and background ornaments.
- The player should understand the scene and the first tap target in under one
  second on a `960x540` screen with UI overlays.

## Background-Only Generation Prompt

```text
Create one real raster 2D illustrated browser RPG background, 1280x700.

Scene: "The Last Post", viewed from inside a living fortified dark-fantasy
frontier city after the ancient Dragon disappeared. The camera is on the city
side of the gate/checkpoint, looking at the inner gate area and nearby streets,
not from outside the walls. It stands on the border and feels суровый/harsh,
guarded, and weather-worn, but it is not ruined, not abandoned, and not a
collapsed settlement. This is the first interactive hub screen, not a splash
screen. Background only.

Composition and layout:
- Master image 1280x700.
- The central game screen is 960x540 at x=160, y=80; all important gameplay
  objects must be inside this central rectangle.
- The left/right 160 px and top/bottom 80 px are decorative bleed only: mist,
  wall continuation, trees, rooftops, cliffs, weathered banners, no important
  objects.
- Inside the central screen, keep top 72 px and bottom 112 px visually quieter
  for future UI overlay.
- Keep the top-left 112x72 area inside the central screen nonessential and
  uncluttered for a Poki button.
- The playable middle band should show a clear interior fortified
  gate/checkpoint and a readable empty guard hotspot on the city side of the
  gate, visible without portrait panning.
- No guard/NPC in the image; the guard will be a separate sprite later.

Visual direction:
- Dark Roblox-like fantasy translated into 2D illustrated browser RPG art.
- Chunky blocky silhouettes, square-ish stone towers, broad wooden beams,
  simple large readable props, low-poly-inspired shapes but painterly 2D.
- Old browser RPG hub readability: clear path, clear clickable object areas,
  warm settlement lights, inhabited city props, dangerous world outside the
  gate only hinted through the gate opening or skyline.
- Mood: dark fantasy, readable and stylized, not realistic, not depressive, not
  post-apocalyptic ruins.
- Palette: charcoal blue and smoky violet shadows, warm amber torches/windows,
  tiny white-gold dragon-memory accents, sparse violet-black cult omen, subtle
  green-black corruption fog beyond the gate.
- Depth: foreground city-side path, crates, barrels, awnings, and storage props;
  midground inner gate/wall/houses; distant exterior threat only as a narrow
  gate view, fog, glow, or skyline.

Hard negatives:
- No text, no UI, no logos, no watermark, no characters, no NPCs, no tutorial
  finger, no speech bubbles, no readable signs, no modern objects, no copied
  Roblox game assets, no exact Vesteria/Deepwoken/World Zero/Darkest Dungeon
  composition, no ruins, no destroyed city, no abandoned settlement, no
  collapsed buildings, no exterior approach shot, no camera outside the walls,
  no painterly micro-detail, no dense stone-by-stone rendering, no cluttered
  market full of tiny props.

Output: a clean game-use background candidate, real raster art, no alpha.
```

## Character Direction

Accepted character rule: NPCs and enemies must match the Roblox-like blocky
mockup language already used in the game screens. They are not realistic fantasy
characters.

For the first guard:

- full-body separate sprite, no baked background;
- square/blocky head, cuboid torso, cuboid arms and legs;
- chunky armor shapes, big boots/gloves, simple stern face;
- dark brown leather and tarnished metal, muted red-brown tabard or cloak;
- readable 3/4 idle silhouette with a narrow vertical spear/halberd;
- warm amber rim light and cool blue-gray shadows to sit over the hub
  background;
- avoid realistic human anatomy, detailed portrait rendering, many straps,
  shiny royal knight language, anime hero proportions, oversized weapon, noisy
  leather grain, scratches, tiny buckles, rivet fields, cloth wrinkles, or
  layered belts.

Guard 01 and Guard 02 were rejected because they drifted into realistic fantasy
character/key-art. Guard 03 corrected the proportions but stayed too noisy.
Guard 04 reduced noise but became too rounded/toy-clean and lost too much of the
Roblox block-rig read. Guard 05 is the current preferred direction: a middle
version with square head, angular cuboid body/limbs, hard planar armor shapes,
contained broad material texture, and stronger first-tap readability.

## Design Gaps

- Exact time of day: dusk, night, or stormy dawn.
- Whether the dormant dragon beacon is visible in the first hub background or
  saved for start/title screen.
- Guard hotspot side: right of gate is recommended, but UX lead may prefer
  center-left depending on tutorial finger placement.
- Amount of cult/green corruption visible in the first minute: recommended low
  and mostly outside the city boundary.
