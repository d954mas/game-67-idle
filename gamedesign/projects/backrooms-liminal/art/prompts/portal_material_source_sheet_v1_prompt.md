# Portal Material Source Sheet v1 Prompt

Use case: stylized-concept
Asset type: game material source sheet for a native 3D Backrooms liminal horror portal room

Create a square 2x2 source sheet containing four clean, cuttable material tiles for a realistic high-quality Backrooms horror game.

- Top-left: aged yellow patterned wallpaper with subtle vertical seams, old adhesive stains, grime, and uneven fluorescent bleaching.
- Top-right: damp low-pile brown-yellow office carpet with matted fibers, darker wet patches, and worn tile seams.
- Bottom-left: stained acoustic drop-ceiling tile material with grid channels, water rings, dust speckles, and sickly fluorescent bounce.
- Bottom-right: aged dark tan wooden/plastic trim and baseboard material with scratches, chipped edges, grime in grooves, and slight glossy wear.

Style/medium: realistic game material source art, painterly-photoreal texture detail, suitable for sampling in a 3D horror game.
Composition/framing: exact 2x2 grid, each quadrant is a distinct square material tile, straight-on orthographic view, no perspective, no labels, no borders thicker than a thin gutter, no shadows between quadrants.
Lighting/mood: sickly fluorescent Backrooms light, dirty, liminal, unsettling, not colorful, not cartoon.
Color palette: yellowed wallpaper, nicotine beige, damp brown carpet, dusty ceiling cream, dark aged tan trim; varied but coherent.
Materials/textures: visible fiber, grime, stains, seams, roughness variation, subtle scratches; avoid flat noise.
Constraints: no text, no letters, no icons, no logos, no watermark, no people, no monsters, no furniture, no UI; keep each quadrant fully filled by its material and easy to crop into 128x128 or 256x256 runtime tiles.
