# Blockside Heat Concept

## Fantasy

Be a blocky street operator in a compact toy-city district: take small risky
jobs, jump into cars, dodge trouble, and turn chaotic city moments into cash and
reputation.

## Hook

The first playable promise is "see a package job, enter a car, grab the package,
and escape while the city reacts."

## Genre And Platform

- Genre: low-poly open-world crime/action sandbox prototype.
- Primary platform: native PC.
- Camera: third-person, readable chase/over-shoulder framing.
- Session: 5-10 minute arcade jobs, starting with a 60-second mission loop.

## Audience

Casual players who understand GTA-like verbs but need simple controls, clear
HUD feedback, toy-like visuals, and readable objectives.

## Pillars

1. One-block clarity: the first district must explain the job, car, route, and
   consequence without a tutorial wall.
2. Toy chaos, not realism: blocky bodies, chunky vehicles, bright city props,
   readable collisions, no gore or gritty simulation.
3. Consequence loop: jobs change cash, wanted level, NPC behavior, and escape
   pressure quickly enough to make replay meaningful.

## No-Go List

- Do not use GTA branding, real brands, copied maps, copied characters, or
  copyrighted UI.
- Do not build a full open world before one block proves movement, car entry,
  objective, NPC reaction, and reward.
- Do not use procedural/shape-renderer art as product visuals except as
  explicitly logged debug debt.
- Do not use handmade pixel/shape text for UI; visible text must use the engine
  text renderer and packed fonts.
- Do not add gore, realistic police violence, or real-world weapon branding.

## Progression Metric

First slice progression is `job_cash`, `wanted_level`, and `job_stage`. Later
progression can add car unlocks, district access, and contact reputation only
after the first job loop passes product-read review.
