# Cozy Automation GDD

## One-Line Concept

A small cozy automation game: place simple producers on a small garden grid; they
generate and auto-route a resource you spend to unlock the next producer/step.

## Audience

Casual players. Progression is clear; controls and moment-to-moment play stay
simple. Readable at a glance, bright and friendly, no fail states.

## Core Loop

1. Watch a producer auto-generate berries that auto-route into your basket.
2. Spend berries on the clear next action (plant another bush) for more output.
3. See output rise and progress climb toward the next unlock.
4. Reach the lock threshold, unlock the greenhouse, and the rate jumps again.

This is **active-session** automation (producers tick while you watch and spend).
It is **not** an idle / away-time / reset-meta loop.

## First Playable Slice — "The Little Garden"

The smallest end-to-end real screen. Exactly one location, one primary action,
one visible-progress readout, one lock.

### Location

A single cozy garden with a row of **3 plots** on grass, a **basket** stockpile
at the top, and a **Plant Bush** action.

### Resource

**Berries** — auto-generated by bushes and auto-routed (a small berry drifts
from each producing bush to the basket on every tick) into the basket counter.

### Producers / Plots

- **Plot 1 — Berry Bush (pre-planted):** producing from the first frame so the
  player immediately sees berries rising. Output: **+1 berry / tick** (~1.5 s).
- **Plot 2 — empty:** the **primary action** — **Plant Bush** for **10 berries**.
  Planting adds **+1 berry / tick** (output doubles).
- **Plot 3 — Greenhouse (LOCKED):** the **one lock**. Shows a padlock and
  "Unlock at 50". Unlocking costs **50 berries** and adds **+3 berries / tick**.

### Visible Progress

- Basket berry **counter** (big, top-center).
- A **progress bar** toward the current next step: `berries / 10` (Plant) →
  then `berries / 50` (Greenhouse) → then "Garden thriving" at max rate.

### Required Reads (product-read gate)

- **Where am I?** A cozy garden with plots, bushes, and a berry basket.
- **What can I do?** Plant a bush (primary CTA) when I can afford it.
- **What changed?** Berries tick up and drift into the basket; planting adds a
  bush and speeds output; the progress bar fills.
- **Why continue?** A locked greenhouse promises a bigger rate jump at 50.
- **Why a game?** Bright, friendly, animated cozy garden — not a debug screen.

### Replay / pull

Optimize toward the greenhouse: every berry spent compounds the rate, so the
garden visibly grows busier and faster — a satisfying short curve.

## Slice Scope / Stop Condition

- One scene, the loop above, real engine text + a small real cozy sprite atlas.
- **Stop** when the first native screenshot passes the product-read gate; do not
  expand producers/grid/economy before that gate.

## Art Direction

Bright, saturated, friendly, readable at a glance. Warm sunny garden palette
(grass greens, soil browns, berry reds/purples, wood basket). Soft rounded
shapes, gentle shadows. Avoid realistic, muddy, low-contrast, or debug looks.
Real engine font for all text; real sprite atlas for garden art (no debug
shape-renderer art in the shipped first screen).

## State (first slice)

- `berries` — int, auto-routed total.
- `plot2_planted` — bool, second bush placed.
- `greenhouse_unlocked` — bool, the lock cleared.

Costs/rates are data, documented in `data/core_loop.json`.
