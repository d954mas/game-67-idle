## Product Pillar

One-line pitch:

> Build one cool modular 3D mech, test it in short readable PvE battles, earn
> salvage/resources, then return to the hangar to buy or craft modules that
> make the mech look and play differently.

Genre:

- Casual mobile/web 3D mech builder battler.
- PvE-first action/build hybrid.
- Accessible progression first, active mastery second.

Audience:

- Casual mobile/web players who want fast progress, visible customization, and
  short combat sessions.
- Players who like mech/robot fantasy but do not want simulator-level build
  complexity or competitive PvP pressure in the first session.

## Design Positioning

This game is not:

- a PvP service shooter;
- a hardcore MechLab simulator;
- a fully automated CATS clone;
- a punch-only robot fighter;
- an idle/incremental game;
- an ad/offer-driven upgrade wall.

This game is:

- a one-mech ownership game;
- a build-battle-upgrade loop;
- a casual action game with semi-automatic targeting;
- a visual progression game where parts change silhouette, effects, and
  playstyle;
- a short-session PvE game that can later grow into events, challenges, or
  asynchronous comparison.

## Core Experience

Player fantasy:

1. I have a mech that is mine.
2. I can quickly understand what to do next.
3. I see my build choice in the model.
4. I test it in a short fight.
5. I get resources or unlock progress.
6. I return to the hangar and choose what to buy, craft, or upgrade.
7. The next fight immediately proves the change.

Emotional cadence:

- Hangar: ownership, anticipation, clear next upgrade.
- Battle: impact, spectacle, readable threat, one tactical decision.
- Reward: relief, visible progress, new toy.
- Upgrade: transformation, comparison, urge to test again.

## First Minute

Target sequence:

1. Player sees one large 3D mech in a hangar.
2. `Battle` is the obvious next action.
3. First fight loads directly into a small arena.
4. Player learns one movement/positioning action and one special action.
5. Enemy takes visible damage with sparks, stagger, shield break, or knockback.
6. Player wins a short tutorial encounter.
7. Reward screen grants salvage/resources.
8. Hangar highlights an affordable first module or craft action.
9. Buying/crafting and equipping changes the mech model or attack effect.
10. Second battle prompt appears immediately.

Hard first-minute no-go list:

- no PvP matchmaking;
- no offer popup stack;
- no full inventory grid;
- no pilot/implant system;
- no long lore intro;
- no text-only stat upgrade;
- no precision aiming requirement;
- no forced ad or wait timer.

## Core Loops

### Moment-To-Moment Loop

Aim: prove the mech feels powerful within 10 seconds.

1. Enemy enters readable range.
2. Mech auto-targets or highlights a target.
3. Player triggers primary/special/defense.
4. Attack lands with strong visual and audio implication.
5. Enemy role reacts: stagger, shield break, explosion, rush interrupt.
6. Heat/energy/cooldown updates.
7. Player repositions or waits for the next opening.

### Battle Loop

Aim: complete one encounter in 60-180 seconds.

1. Enter arena.
2. Clear a small wave or boss phase.
3. Use one build-defining action.
4. Collect salvage/resources.
5. See concise result screen.
6. Return to hangar.

### Session Loop

Aim: finish a satisfying casual session in 3-7 minutes.

1. Complete 2-3 encounters.
2. Upgrade or swap one part.
3. Unlock one mission node, enemy, or part family.
4. See a visible before/after on the mech.
5. Choose: push harder, farm known mission, or test alternate part.

## Control Model

Recommended first implementation target: semi-auto arena.

Controls:

- Movement: floating virtual joystick / drag movement zone on mobile target;
  WASD in the native PC harness.
- Targeting: automatic nearest/high-priority target.
- Primary attack: automatic or single large button.
- Special: one loadout-defining button.
- Defense/mobility: one short dash button.
- Camera: fixed three-quarter/isometric combat view tuned for phone readability.

Why this wins:

- It preserves the feeling that the player pilots a mech.
- It avoids precise shooter aiming.
- It gives buildcraft room to matter.
- It is simpler than Mech Arena and more active than CATS.

Fallback control option:

- Tactical auto-battler: movement/basic attack automatic, player triggers
  special/defense. Use only if early input tests show movement is too much for
  mobile/web.

Rejected first-slice option:

- Fully automated build simulation. It is accessible, but too weak for the
  user's desired battles, 3D spectacle, and mech-pilot expectation.

## Mech Assembly

First-slice slots:

| Slot | Player Reads It As | Gameplay Meaning | Visual Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core/body | what my mech is | health, heat/energy, weight class | torso, cockpit/sensor, reactor glow |
| Weapon | how I attack | primary pattern and range | arm cannon, blade, laser emitter |
| Legs/drive | how I move | speed, dash, stability, knockback | feet, pistons, boosters |
| Special module | my trick | rockets, shield, drone, repair, finisher | shoulder pod, shield plate, drone bay |
| Paint/decal | expression | cosmetic reward only | color, markings, small badges |

Part rules:

- Every mechanical part must change model silhouette or combat effect.
- Numeric upgrade can exist, but milestone upgrades should add behavior.
- Slots must be readable before the inventory becomes deep.
- `Equip best` can help casual players, but the UI must show why the part is
  recommended.
- Cosmetic rewards should never pretend to be required power progression.

## Build Archetypes

Use archetypes to keep customization understandable:

### Starter Striker

- Weapon: arm cannon.
- Legs: balanced drive.
- Special: short dash.
- Role: first tutorial baseline.
- Teaches: movement, attack, cooldown.

### Missile Breaker

- Weapon: medium cannon or light blaster.
- Legs: heavy stable legs.
- Special: shoulder rockets.
- Role: clears swarms and shield guards.
- Teaches: heat timing and area burst.

### Shield Bruiser

- Weapon: short-range cannon or blade.
- Legs: heavy legs.
- Special: shield bash or frontal guard.
- Role: survives chargers and mini-boss bursts.
- Teaches: defensive timing.

### Laser Ranger

- Weapon: laser/beam emitter.
- Legs: light or jump legs.
- Special: focus shot or weak-point mark.
- Role: counters turrets/snipers.
- Teaches: positioning and line safety.

First playable only needs three loadouts: Starter Striker, Missile Breaker, and
Shield Bruiser.

## Combat

### Combat Principles

- The player should win by understanding enemy role and using the equipped
  part, not by reading small stats.
- Battles must be short enough to replay after changing a part.
- Enemy attacks must be telegraphed.
- The mech should stay visible through effects.
- Difficulty should grow through roles and combinations before HP inflation.

### Resources In Battle

Use one tactical limiter first:

- Heat mechanics with `Cooling` UI label: weapons and specials generate heat;
  overheating delays actions, while the UI frames the wait as cooling down.

Why heat:

- It feels mechanical.
- It can be visualized through vents, glow, warning color, and cooldown.
- It gives heavy weapons a casual timing decision without ammo complexity.

Possible later alternative:

- Energy: shield/dash/special use shared energy. Easier to explain, less mech
  specific than heat.

### Enemy Roles

First-slice enemy set:

| Enemy | Purpose | Counter |
|---|---|---|
| Drone swarm | teaches area damage and target priority | rockets, sweeping shot |
| Shield guard | teaches armor and break/flank | heavy hit, shield bash, melee |
| Sniper turret | teaches danger telegraph | dash, line break, laser counter |
| Charger | teaches timing and defense | shield, brace, interrupt |
| Mini-boss | tests the full current build | use weapon + special + defense |

## Progression And Meta

Primary meta loop:

Battle -> salvage/resources -> buy/craft module or progress blueprint ->
equip/upgrade -> unlock mission -> battle.

Currencies:

- Salvage: common upgrade currency.
- Blueprints: deterministic unlock progress for part families.
- Core cells: rare milestone upgrade material for core/body.
- Paint chips: cosmetic progression.

Rules:

- Keep currencies few.
- Every resource must explain a player decision.
- Random rewards can add surprise, but deterministic targeting must exist.
- No randomized reward path should be the only way to complete a build
  archetype.

Upgrade milestones:

- Level 1-2: stat clarity and basic feel improvement.
- Level 3: visible behavior change, such as extra rocket, wider shield, faster
  dash, heat refund, or stagger bonus.
- Level 5: archetype identity upgrade, such as burn trail, drone split, shield
  counter, or weak-point mark.

## Mission Structure

First map shape:

- Linear mission path with optional replay.
- Each node teaches one enemy role or part use.
- Mini-boss appears after 2-3 short encounters.
- Harder versions unlock after the first loop is understood.

Mission examples:

1. Scrap Yard Gate: drones only, teaches attack and salvage.
2. Shield Blockade: shield guard, teaches special/heavy hit.
3. Rail Turret Yard: turret danger line, teaches dash/cover.
4. Loader Ambush: charger, teaches defense timing.
5. Foundry Warden: mini-boss, tests current build.

## Screens

### Hangar

Purpose:

- Ownership, upgrade, next action.

Required elements:

- large 3D mech;
- `Battle` primary action;
- 3-5 visible part slots;
- one upgrade/equip prompt;
- locked/progress hint;
- minimal resource counters.

### Battle

Purpose:

- Prove the current build.

Required elements:

- readable player mech;
- one or more enemy roles;
- health/progress at top;
- movement area on left or tap space;
- action buttons on right;
- heat/cooldown feedback;
- reward pickup/feedback.

### Reward

Purpose:

- Explain what changed and where it goes.

Required elements:

- salvage amount;
- affordable module/craft option or blueprint progress;
- slot destination for the chosen module;
- `Buy`, `Craft`, `Equip`, or `Upgrade` action;
- next battle prompt after equip.

### Upgrade

Purpose:

- Convert reward into visible change.

Required elements:

- before/after preview;
- one meaningful stat or behavior callout;
- model/effect change;
- `Test` or `Battle` action.

## Visual Direction

Visual target:

- Stylized 3D mobile action, not gritty sim realism.
- Chunky modular mech, readable at phone scale.
- Calm industrial hangar, bright functional combat feedback.
- Enemy silhouettes explain role before labels.
- Effects communicate hit, shield, heat, reward, and upgrade.
- The genre expectation is a bright, juicy 3D mech image. Lighting, shadow
  contact, normal/detail response, bevels, painted metal, emissive reactor
  accents, and readable mechanical silhouettes are core requirements.
- First playable should use real/model-like GLB assets or generated/kitbashed
  models as soon as possible. Shape/debug renderers may support engineering,
  but they are not acceptable as the visual target.

See [visual reference packet](../references/visual_reference_packet_2026-06-19.md)
for the current detailed art-direction constraints.

## Economy And Monetization Guardrails

This GDD does not design monetization. It records constraints from reference
risks:

- No forced monetization before first complete build-battle-upgrade loop.
- No pay-only counter for normal PvE progression.
- No offer popup stack on session start.
- No forced ad as the expected way to pass a difficulty spike.
- No power creep that makes recent player investment feel immediately obsolete.
- Rewarded ads, if ever added, must be optional acceleration or extra reward,
  not core progression.

## MVP / First Vertical Slice

The first playable proof should contain:

- one hangar screen;
- one player mech;
- three part/loadout configurations;
- one small battle arena;
- four enemy roles plus one mini-boss;
- salvage/resources and one guided first purchase/craft module;
- one equip/upgrade flow;
- screenshot proof for hangar, battle, special effect, reward, and upgraded
  hangar.

First playable content:

- Loadouts: Starter Striker, Missile Breaker, Shield Bruiser.
- Enemies: drone swarm, shield guard, charger, mini-boss.
- Optional if cheap: sniper turret as the fourth role.

Definition of playable proof:

- The player can understand the next action on the first screen.
- The first battle can be completed without reading a manual.
- Equipping a part visibly changes model or effect.
- The second battle proves that change.
- The screenshot reads as a game, not a debug tool.

## Risks And Gates

Accepted decisions:

- Native PC slice scope with mobile-style controls/readability.
- Later web/mobile export path.
- Landscape-first orientation.
- Semi-auto arena vs tactical auto-battler after input testing.
- Industrial salvage sport tone with bright accents.
- Fixed three-quarter/isometric camera.
- Foundry Warden industrial mini-boss.

Remaining gates:

- Accepted visual fake shot for final art polish.
- Stronger source evidence for exact UI/economy patterns.

Implementation gates:

- Do not start runtime implementation until the native PC slice scope is
  accepted.
- Do not start web/mobile export or frontend/runtime-path work without explicit
  approval.
- Do not generate final art until fake shot or accepted visual target exists.
- Do not claim exact reference grounding for UI/economy/combat until source
  gaps in the relevant deconstruction are closed or explicitly accepted.
- Keep PvP out of the first version.
