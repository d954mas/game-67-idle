## Observed Visual Facts

- Roblox mech worlds read best when the environment is simple and bright:
  baseplates, block platforms, studded/checker ground, sky color, and a few
  large props instead of dark industrial noise.
- The mech can be blocky, but the blockiness must be intentional: large torso,
  oversized shoulders/arms/feet, one readable weapon, obvious colored modules,
  and strong separation from the world.
- Scale is sold by contrast: tiny Roblox-sized pilot/avatar or cockpit vs. huge
  mech/boss, plus simple oversized health bars or labels.
- Materials are not realistic metal first. They are toy-like plastic/painted
  parts with bright color bands, emissive accents, and clean silhouettes.
- Controls should be casual and explicit: WASD on PC, mobile joystick/buttons
  later, dash/boost, primary attack, secondary/special. Movement clarity matters
  more than simulation depth.
- A baseplate can be more unique than ordinary studs: combine a stylized ground
  motif, raised/semi-transparent studs, and deliberate gaps around the motif so
  the surface reads custom rather than default Roblox.

## Borrow

- Bright block world: green/gray baseplate, block ramps/platforms, simple sky,
  chunky props, clear arena boundary.
- Stylized studs surface: grassy or tech pattern under a studs grid, with
  readable raised studs but enough motif visibility to avoid a generic grid.
- Toy mech proportions: big feet, big shoulders, visible cockpit/head, broad
  torso, chunky weapon pods.
- Build identity: each module should look attachable: shoulder pod, arm cannon,
  booster, leg block, core block.
- Simple HUD: health/cooling/salvage in high-contrast game font; large buttons;
  no dense sci-fi panels.

## Avoid

- Do not copy Roblox branding, exact game names, licensed Iron Man/Gundam forms,
  or a specific developer's mech silhouette.
- Do not chase realistic bevel-heavy mech fidelity before the Roblox-like toy
  grammar lands.
- Do not make the world dark, gridded, or industrial if the target is now
  Roblox-like readability.
- Do not leave the player as a small unclear block pile. The mech must be a
  deliberate toy machine with a primary silhouette.

## Current Build Mismatch

- First Roblox-like corrective pass has removed the dark industrial arena read:
  the native scene now uses sky color, bright block baseplate, studs, decorative
  grass motifs, and simple colored block props.
- Mech now uses a downloaded CC0 Poly Pizza/Quaternius source model as its
  visible silhouette, so it reads much closer to a Roblox-like toy mech/suit
  than the previous procedural block assembly.
- Shoulder hardpoints now visibly change state: locked orange sockets before
  purchase, amber/cyan rocket pods after purchase.
- UI is readable and uses engine text, but still closer to prototype HUD than
  playful Roblox-like toy action UI.
