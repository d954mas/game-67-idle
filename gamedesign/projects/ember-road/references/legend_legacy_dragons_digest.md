## Observed Facts

- Observed: city/hub screens use a large illustrated place panel framed by
  ornate fantasy chrome, with navigation/status surfaces around it.
- Observed: the inventory/status surface is dense: equipment slots, item grid,
  tabs, currency counters, and item tooltip can coexist on one screen.
- Observed: hunt/map mode shows entity labels and levels directly in the play
  field, with top action icons and a compass.
- Observed: world-map screenshots emphasize painted terrain, routes, and
  highlighted destinations rather than a plain node list.
- Observed: side menus can group locations, objects, NPCs, quests, professions,
  and travel readiness in one ornate panel.

## Borrow / Avoid / Copy-Risk

- borrow: fantasy hero progression, dense quest/map RPG feeling, ornate fantasy
  UI intent, illustrated location panel, visible hero status/rewards, map/town
  travel fantasy, side-menu destination grouping, inventory/equipment/status
  density.
- avoid: copying exact names, factions, characters, screenshots, icon shapes,
  protected UI layout, monetization pressure, or confusing old-browser clutter.
- copy-risk: any direct visual reconstruction of reference screens, item art,
  map art, faction symbols, typography, quest text, or battle presentation.
