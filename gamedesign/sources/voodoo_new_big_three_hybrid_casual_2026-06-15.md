## Research question

What casual/hybrid-casual genres, core moments, and monetization patterns are
actually winning in 2026, to pick and shape the next small game prototype?

## Thesis

VOODOO (the hypercasual era's poster child, IAA-first) now has three 2026 hits
driven by IAP, not ads. Apr-May 2026 IAP: Castle Busters >$10M, Marble Sort!
~$9M, Sand Loop >$5M. Two of the three are loop-based Sort Puzzles; one is a
midcore PvP brawler. The pattern: catchy concept + a satisfying ("ASMR") core
loop + deeper hybrid-casual monetization, and scaling a proven formula across
themes.

## The three games (facts)

| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Castle Busters | Midcore turn-based PvP brawler | May 2025 | >19.7M (5.8M/mo) | >$14.8M | Destructible castles, squad drafting, short matches. "Angry Birds x Brawl Stars," overt Supercell fingerprints. Hit #11 Midcore by downloads in a month, out-downloaded Brawl Stars that month. |

Genre context: the loop/conveyor Sort Puzzle wave was popularized by Pixel
Flow! (>$100M IAP, ~$1B Scopely acquisition). VOODOO runs a whole Sort Puzzle
lineup and replicates the formula across themes (marbles, sand, ...).

## What is working (observed)

- Satisfying / ASMR core moment SELLS. Marbles roll/click/tumble; sand melts and
  distorts. "Satisfying sells" is stated as the operative rule.
- Learn-in-seconds, depth-later: trivial first action, then tighter spaces, new
  mechanics, chaotic layouts.
- One small twist on a familiar genre makes it feel fresh (thrown-marble physics)
  - not reinvention.
- Hybrid-casual monetization (IAP-led, ads support):
  - One dominant pack carries revenue (Marble Sort Pack = 85.5% of revenue,
    86.9% of purchases).
  - No-Ads offers (~3.7% in Marble Sort!), fail offers tied to difficulty
    (~1.9%), streak bonuses (doubled coins for returning), limited-time events
    (Sugar Rush; Castle Busters' Wizard's Bargain / tournaments), passes (Forge
    Pass), tiered bundles, upgrade timers (pressure -> shortcuts).
  - Rewarded ads are a SUPPORT lever (speed-ups, extra resources), not the
    load-bearer; older ad-led titles (Epic Plane) spiked then dropped off.
- Fast onboarding + near-constant rewards + always "one more session" hooks
  (daily streaks/chests, seasonal events, long-term unlocks).
- Scale a proven concept across themes; "big three -> four -> five."

## Borrow (for our prototype)

- Pick a core loop whose single action is intrinsically SATISFYING in motion
  (physics, ASMR) - matches our own "core moment must be a felt reveal" rule.
- Learn-in-seconds first screen; add depth after the loop reads.
- One small mechanical twist on a proven genre, not a new genre from scratch.
- Plan monetization as one dominant pack + No-Ads + difficulty-driven fail
  offers; rewarded ads as support (only when we get to a web/mobile target).

## Avoid / copy-risk

- Do not clone a specific title's content/IP (Castle Busters' overt Brawl Stars
  borrowing is theirs; we take the structural lesson, not the assets/identity).
- Do not lead with ad-spam monetization (burns retention).
- Figures are estimates; treat as direction, not exact truth.
