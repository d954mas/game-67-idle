# T0256 — Text-to-Animation, COMMUNITY-PRACTICE angle (what people actually use/recommend)

Date 2026-07-04. Author deep-reasoner. Scope: web+GitHub receipts on the *converged* stack for I2V on a 12GB box and for game-sprite animation specifically. Sibling generative report = local WAN 2.2 I2V + MatAnyone; this note confirms/adjusts it with community evidence. [H]=hearsay/sentiment, not a hard receipt.

- **Local 12GB consensus IS WAN 2.2 I2V, GGUF Q4 + a 4-step Lightning/LightX2V LoRA, via ComfyUI native workflow** — exactly the sibling's pick, and it is the battle-tested default. Community reports ~4.6 min for 1s@15fps on a *6GB* RTX 3050 (github.com/Cordux/ComfyUI-Wan2.2-workflow, 34★, 4-step GGUF) and "Q4 for 12GB" is the standard quant guidance (nextdiffusion.ai/wan22-image-to-video-gguf; docs.comfy.org/tutorials/video/wan/wan2_2; blog.comfy.org LightX2V 4-step). Realistic 12GB throughput = minutes-per-clip, not real-time — Lightning LoRA is the mandatory enabler, matching sibling R3.
- **Fresher entrants to benchmark, don't blindly stay on 2.2:** HunyuanVideo-1.5 (Tencent, 8.3B, Nov-2025, "runs on consumer GPUs" — github.com/Tencent-Hunyuan/HunyuanVideo-1.5) is the notable lightweight I2V; LTX-2.3 (Lightricks, Mar-2026) is the speed/draft option (~real-time on a 4090). WAN 2.5/2.6/3.0/2.7 appear only in content-farm SEO blogs (mindstudio, wavespeed) and 2.5 launched API-only/no open weights — treat as **unverified**; 2.2 remains the one with the mature LoRA/GGUF/workflow ecosystem.
- **Anti-recommendations (dead branches):** AnimateDiff and SVD are *not* dead but the community has demoted them to short ambient/loop motion on low VRAM; nobody recommends them for identity-preserving sprite motion in 2026 (fluxnote.io animate-diff-vs-stable-video; hardwarepedia 2026 guide) [H]. FramePack (lllyasviel, Apr-2025, 6GB, long video) is a real niche tool but oriented to *length*, not sprite fidelity — skip for v1.
- **GAMEDEV VERDICT — the big adjustment: shipped sprite tools do NOT use local I2V+matting.** They converged on **image-model chat-consistency + per-frame background removal**, with I2V as a narrow accessory. Receipts: github.com/chongdashu/ai-game-spritesheets (102★) uses GPT-Image-2.0 for anchors and Seedance I2V *only* for walk cycles; github.com/lovisdotio/falsprite (187★) = nano-banana-2 stills + BRIA bg-removal; Scenario/Rosebud/Robotic-Ape "Lessons Learned" (roboticape.com 2026-03-07) all lean on **Nano Banana / Nano Banana Pro (Gemini)**: "character consistency is exceptionally high… idle/walk generate perfectly, but complex combos break consistency." So I2V-for-stylized-sprites is still **niche/painful for identity** — the practitioner move is image-model-first, or the hybrid "make a sprite sheet with Nano Banana, feed it as an ingredient to Veo 3.1 to hold temporal consistency" (x.com/nmatares 2025-11). Style-drift/photorealization of I2V on stylized art is why they avoid pure I2V [H, inferred from tool choices, no clean r/SD quote found].
- **Loops (proven in shared workflows):** feed the *same* image as first+last frame in WAN's FLF node and drop the duplicate last frame → seamless loop (nextdiffusion.ai/wan-2-2-looping-animations); ping-pong/boomerang via VHS `pingpong=true` (github.com/Kosinkadink/ComfyUI-VideoHelperSuite) — both are standard, low-risk. Confirms sibling's loop plan.
- **Alpha in real practice, two camps.** (1) Video matting for opaque clips — **MatAnyone is the maintained community pick** (now v2/CVPR-2026 nodes, any-bg, flicker-free; runcomfy MatAnyone); Fannovel16/ComfyUI-Video-Matting (227★, RVM+RMBG) is the older lightweight fallback. (2) But the sprite-tool camp above skips temporal matting and just runs **per-frame BG removal (BRIA/remove.bg/RMBG/BiRefNet)** — simpler, ships today, accepts mild flicker. **CorridorKey verdict so far = positive & fast-adopting** (Niko Pueringer/Corridor, Mar-2026; hackaday.com 2026-03-18; creativeainews): neural un-multiply → clean linear alpha incl. motion blur/translucency, community optimized 96GB→**6-8GB VRAM**, EZ-CorridorKey GUI exists — a legit fresh alternative to MatAnyone for the glow/hair case, still brand-new so treat as "watch + trial."
- **Iteration reality:** the community frames re-generation as **"gacha/reroll"** — batch variants, filter the best — because I2V motion is stochastic (help.apiyi.com sora gacha guide). Sentiment consensus is "prompt gets ~80%, then you hand-fix / switch tools," not "iterate purely by editing the prompt" [H, no single hard source]. **Implication for the lead's "исправить проблемы" loop:** don't sell prompt-only iteration on the generative route — pin identity with the first frame + fixed seed, expose motion-strength/seed/denoise as knobs, and expect a re-roll gate; this is exactly why the *procedural* sibling's editable-parameter route wins the daily "nudge, don't re-roll" case.

## 3-5 concrete recommendations (adjust/confirm sibling generative plan)
1. **CONFIRM** WAN 2.2 I2V GGUF Q4 + 4-step Lightning as the local engine — it is the community standard for this VRAM class; but **add HunyuanVideo-1.5 and LTX-2.3 to the R1 bench** rather than committing to WAN 2.2 as the sole engine.
2. **ADJUST the gamedev framing:** for identity-critical *sprite* frames the shipped-tool consensus is **image-model-first (Nano Banana/GPT-Image) + per-frame BG-removal**, not local I2V+matting. Recommend a cheaper v1 "sprite" lane = his existing gpt-image stills → BiRefNet/RMBG per frame, and reserve WAN-I2V+MatAnyone for *organic one-shot* motion (glow, fluid, creature) where a still-sheet can't express intent — this dovetails with the procedural sibling's territory split.
3. **CONFIRM alpha via MatAnyone**, and **add CorridorKey to the alpha bench** (fresh, now 6-8GB, strong on motion-blur/translucency) — could beat MatAnyone on the glow case the sibling flagged as its weak point.
4. **CONFIRM loops** = FLF same-image + drop-last-frame, ping-pong fallback (both community-proven).
5. **Set iteration expectations honestly:** market the generative route as first-frame+seed-pinned with a re-roll gate ("gacha"), and route the everyday "махать медленнее / поправить" edits to the procedural/parameter path — the community does not iterate stylized I2V by prompt alone.
